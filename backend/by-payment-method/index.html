refund Reversal
1. Partial Capture-When the payment of a certain amount is authorized, some financial service providers offer a one-off deposit of less than the authorized amount. If e.g. the price of the item the consumer ordered is reduced. Not to be confused with a split capture.
2. Split capture-When the payment of a certain amount is authorized, some financial service providers offer to deposit the total amount not in one single deposit but in multiple smaller deposits. E.g. when shipping only some items of the consumer’s shopping basket then make deposits for each separate shipping up to the total authorized amount (e.g. 100 authorized, 2×50 deposited). Not to be confused with partial capture.
3. Has to be authorized by the respective financial service provider.
4. This operation cancels a credit note and can only be done before the corresponding day-end closing has been executed and the financial service provider supports this operation.
5. One step reversal-A deposit reversal results in a complete cancellation of the payment and not in the status approved.
6. The feature set depends on the aktive and used credit card acquirer. All acquirer can be found on this page
7. By default, this payment is automatically captured with full amount. Contact our integration specialists team for separating the transaction flow into authorization and capture (Full or partial capture [1]).
8. recurPayment is affected as payment method SEPA Direct Debit, i.e. supporting SEPA Direct Debit is must.
